Over 600,000 people die of heart conditions every year in the USA. Lisinopril is a relatively inexpensive prescription medication used to treat high blood pressure and congestive heart failure. It can also increase the chances of survival after a heart attack. Lisinopril belongs to a class of drugs called ACE (angiotensin converting enzyme) inhibitors. Enzymes, like Angiotensin II, are responsible for helping arteries contract, thus increasing blood pressure and forcing the heart to work harder. ACE inhibitors reduce the effectiveness of these enzymes and, thus, lower blood pressure. Because the heart has to work less to pump blood, ACE inhibitors also help conditions like heart failure.
